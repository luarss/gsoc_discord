### 14:53 - @Martin Povišer

<@1383173578899460266> to your questions:

What moves already exist?
 - Unbuffer: removes a buffer on a critical path if that improves timing
 - VT swap: swaps out the VT variant of a cell
 - Size up: swaps out a cell for a drive strength better matching its load
 - Swap pins: swaps the input pins on a commutative gate such as AND or XOR
 - Buffer: inserts buffers
 - Split Load: also inserts buffers but with different criteria
 - Clone: clones gates to reduce load
You can see the code at https://github.com/The-OpenROAD-Project/OpenROAD/tree/master/src/rsz/src. Moves are implemented in classes deriving from BaseMove

What designs should I benchmark on?
Eventually we will benchmark on all ORFS designs. ibex/aes as two representatives sound good for intial testing.

What's the bar for enabling?
We will look at the QoR impact across the ORFS design suite and check if on average enabling the move is an improvement, and there are no severe degradations on any particular design.

- AdjustSkew: adjust the clock skew of a register to launch its output earlier or sample its input later
